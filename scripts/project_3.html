Project-3: Multilingual LLM Chatbot for Cancer Patients and Caregivers

Overview

This project aims to develop a custom Large Language Model (LLM) chatbot capable of communicating with users in English, Hindi, Telugu, and Odia. The chatbot is designed to assist cancer patients and their caregivers by providing information, answering questions, and facilitating communication with the doctor's office. In the future, more languages will be added to enhance the chatbot's accessibility.

Objectives

  • Develop a multilingual LLM chatbot.
  • Enable the chatbot to provide support and answer questions in English, Hindi, Telugu, and Odia.
  • Facilitate communication between cancer patients/caregivers and the doctor's office.
  • Plan for the inclusion of additional languages in the future.

Methodology

  1. Data Collection

    Gather data for training the LLM, including medical terminology, common patient questions, and responses in the target languages.

  2. Model Training

    Use state-of-the-art NLP frameworks to train a multilingual LLM capable of understanding and generating text in the specified languages.

  3. Chatbot Development

    Develop the chatbot interface and integrate it with the trained LLM model. Ensure it can handle language detection and appropriate responses.

  4. Testing and Validation

    Test the chatbot with real users to validate its performance and accuracy in different languages. Collect feedback for improvements.

  5. Deployment

    Deploy the chatbot on a web platform accessible to users. Implement mechanisms for continuous learning and updates.

Results

The outcomes of this project include:

  • A functional multilingual chatbot that supports English, Hindi, Telugu, and Odia.
  • Improved communication between cancer patients/caregivers and healthcare providers.
  • A plan for future expansion to include more languages.
